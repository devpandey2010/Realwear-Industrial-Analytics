**Project Title**: 
RealWear Industrial Operations Analytics & Smart Factory Intelligence System

**Industry Context**:

Modern manufacturing plants increasingly rely on wearable technology to enable remote assistance, real-time communication, and hands-free operations on the shop floor. RealWear Navigator 500 devices are deployed across 5 industrial plants in India — Chennai Refinery Unit, Jamshedpur Steel Plant, Bokaro Manufacturing Hub, Pune Automation Plant, and Vadodara Chemical Unit — to facilitate voice-command-driven remote sessions between field workers and expert teams.

**The Problem**:

Despite widespread deployment, the operational data from these devices reveals significant inefficiencies. Workers across different plants, departments, and shifts experience varying levels of command failures, session drops, unresolved issues, and device degradation — yet there is no unified system to monitor, analyze, or predict these problems. Management currently lacks visibility into which plants underperform, which communication platform (MS Teams vs Webex) is more effective, how noise levels impact voice command accuracy, and which devices are at risk of failure.

**What This Project Will Do**:

This project performs an end-to-end analysis of 12,000 operational sessions recorded across 20 RealWear devices from January 2025 to January 2026. Using Excel, SQL, Power BI, Python, Statistical Analysis, and Machine Learning, the project will identify productivity bottlenecks, evaluate platform and device performance, detect failure patterns, and build predictive models for command failures, issue resolution time, and downtime risk — ultimately delivering actionable recommendations to optimize industrial remote assistance operations.
